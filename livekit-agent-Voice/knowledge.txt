# Plant Disease Diagnosis Knowledge Base

## Black Spot Disease (Rose Black Spot)
- **Symptoms**: Yellow leaves, dark spots (black or dark brown) on the upper sides of leaves. The edges of the spots are often fringed or feathery. Leaves may drop prematurely.
- **Affected Plants**: Roses and other ornamental plants.
- **Treatment**: Remove and destroy infected leaves. Ensure good air circulation and avoid overhead watering. Apply fungicides such as neem oil, sulfur, or copper-based sprays.

## Powdery Mildew
- **Symptoms**: White powdery coating on leaves, stems, and flowers. Leaf curling, stunted growth.
- **Affected Plants**: Many, including squash, cucumbers, roses.
- **Treatment**: Improve air circulation, spray with baking soda solution or neem oil.

## Blight (Early / Late Blight)
- **Symptoms**: Brown lesions with yellow halos on lower leaves. Stem cankers and fruit rot in later stages.
- **Affected Plants**: Tomatoes, potatoes.
- **Treatment**: Apply copper fungicides. Ensure crop rotation and remove infected debris.
